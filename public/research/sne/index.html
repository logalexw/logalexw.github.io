core-collapse supernovae observables

Core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe; SNe IIp) serve as the endpoints of the long lives of massive stars and the formative moments of young compact objects, such as black holes and neutron stars. In the data driven world of multimessenger astronomy, exploring how observations connect to theory-driven simulations is key to our understanding. I am working to develop a comprehensive statistical analysis of lightcurve models to determine supernovae simulation accuracy with Carla Fröhlich at North Carolina State University.

Using the stellar evolution models presented in Sukhbold+16, I have modeled early timescale CCSNe hydrodynamics using PUSH, calculated detailed nucleosynthesis for all exploding models using a 2000+ isotope nuclear reaction network, and produced lightcurves .
